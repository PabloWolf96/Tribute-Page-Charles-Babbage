Charles Babbage

The father of computer

Here is a time line of Charles Babbage's life

  • 1791 - Born in London, Southwark
  • 1808 - Family moved to the old Rowdens house in East Teignmouth
  • 1810 - Arrived at Trinty College Cambridge
  • 1812 - Formed the Analytical Society with John Herschel and George Peacock
  • 1812 - Transferred to Peterhouse, Cambridge
  • 1814 - Received a degree without examination
  • 1815 - Lectured the Royal Institution on astronomy
  • 1816 - Elected a fellow of the Royal Society
  • 1819 - Visited Paris and the Society of Arcueil
  • 1822 - Started to work on the difference machine
  • 1825 - Published work on the electrodynamics of Arago's rotations
  • 1827 - Inherited $100,000 after his father's death
  • 1827 - Death of wife
  • 1838 - Invented the Pilot
  • 1871 - Death
